üîê About the Law

Cyberbullying is addressed under multiple Indian laws including the Information Technology Act, 2000 (Section 66A, 66E, 67) and the Indian Penal Code (IPC Sections 354D, 509, 507). These laws protect individuals from online harassment, threats, defamation, and privacy invasion on social media or digital platforms.

üö® Steps to Take If You're a Victim

  • Do not delete evidence ‚Äì take screenshots of chats, emails, or posts.
  • Block the abuser from all platforms and note down usernames or IDs.
  • Report the incident on the platform (Instagram, Facebook, etc.)
  • File an online complaint at cybercrime.gov.in or visit the nearest cyber cell or police station.

üìé Documents Required

  • ID Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.)
  • Screenshots of offensive messages/posts
  • Details of the accused (username, profile link, contact if known)
  • Proof of psychological distress (optional ‚Äì medical report)
