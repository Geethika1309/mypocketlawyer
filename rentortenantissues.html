Rent / Tenant Disputes

Overview

Rent and tenant issues in India are governed primarily by state-specific Rent Control Acts. These laws aim to balance the rights of both landlords and tenants. Common problems include illegal eviction, non-payment of rent, lack of rent agreement, excessive rent hikes, and denial of basic facilities.

Your Legal Rights as a Tenant

As a tenant, you have the right to:

  • Receive a written notice before eviction.
  • Live in a property that meets basic living conditions.
  • Refuse arbitrary rent hikes without due notice.
  • Receive rent receipts and access to utilities.
  • Be protected against harassment or threats from the landlord.

What To Do If You're Facing Issues

If you‚Äôre facing issues like wrongful eviction, non-return of security deposit, or harassment, follow these steps:

  • Speak to your landlord and try to resolve the matter amicably.
  • Send a legal notice if issues persist.
  • File a complaint with the Rent Controller or Consumer Forum.
  • Document all communication, payment receipts, and rental agreements.

Documents Required for Complaint

  • Copy of the Rental Agreement
  • Rent payment receipts or bank statements
  • Copy of legal notice (if any)
  • Proof of identity and address
  • Photos/videos of property condition (if applicable)
